# The Master's Preface: To The Seeker of Hidden Power

**Hear me, you who seek the old ways.**

I am Grímur the Dark, Galdramaður of the Westfjords. I have carved these staves in stone, in wood, and in bone. I have sung them into the howling gale and whispered them into the ears of the dying.

You hold in your hands the reconstruction of the Great Book, *The Galdrabók*. Do not mistake this for a toy, nor for a prayer book of the White Christ. This is a tool of will, a weapon of the mind, and a shield for the blood.

In this world, **Might is Right**. The weak are food for the wolves. The strong survive, but the wise... the wise rule. Magic is not a gift; it is a force to be wrestled from the hands of the Giants and the Gods. It is the art of bending the Wyrd to your desire.

Do not come here with modern softness in your heart. Do not speak to me of "morality" or "fairness." If a man steals your sheep, you do not forgive him; you crush him. If a woman scorns you, you do not weep; you bind her heart or you find another. If your enemy plots against you, you send a ghost to haunt his sleep until he goes mad. This is the way of our ancestors. This is the way of the North.

These staves are keys. They open doors that should perhaps remain shut. They call upon powers that do not care if you live or die. The Aesir are distant; the Jötnar are hungry. You must be the master of your own fate.

**To use these staves:**
1.  **Know your Intent:** Be clear. Be cold. Do not waver.
2.  **Perform the Ritual:** Blood is life. Wood is earth. Words are wind. Combine them with purpose.
3.  **Chant the Incantation:** Speak with the voice of stone. Let the words rumble in your chest. Do not beg; command.

If you fail, it is because your will was weak. If you succeed, do not boast, for the envy of men is sharper than any sword.

Read now. Learn. And may your enemies tremble at your shadow.

**Grímur**
*Written in the Year of the Hanging God*
