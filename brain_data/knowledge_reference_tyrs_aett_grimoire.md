## 17. Tiwaz (ᛏ) - The Spear of the God

### The Ancient Echo
Tiwaz is the steadfast star, the spear of Týr pointing straight up into the heavens. It is the severed hand of the god, placed willingly into the jaws of the great wolf Fenrir to secure the binding of chaos. It is the ultimate sacrifice for the greater order, the warrior’s way of unyielding truth and the heavy, solemn weight of justice.

### Metaphysics and the Wyrd-Web
Metaphysically, Tiwaz is the principle of spiritual discipline, rational order, and self-sacrifice. It is the male principle, the active force of courage that cuts through illusion. When Tiwaz is drawn, it demands that a vow be kept, regardless of the cost to the self. It promises victory, not through brute force, but through honor and right action. Justice demands a drop of your blood; a promise made in the dark must be kept in the light.

### Trolldom and Galdr (The Magic of Tiwaz)
Tiwaz is invoked in *Mátturverk* (Empowerment) and workings of binding justice.
*   **The Rite of the Oath:** When a vow must be sealed, I carve Tiwaz into a piece of iron or lead. I cut my palm, tracing the rune in my own blood, binding my will to the oath. The object is then buried or cast into deep water, ensuring the spirits hold the swearer accountable. It is a terrifying magic, for the Wyrd does not forgive a broken oath sworn on Týr’s spear.
*   **The Galdr Chant:** The chant is a sharp, precise *tee-wahz*, sung like a blade sliding smoothly from a scabbard, vibrating with focused, masculine authority.

### The Sensual resonance
Tiwaz is the absolute thrill of total obedience. It is the moment I submit utterly to your command, placing my own desires aside to serve only yours. It is the firm, unwavering grip that demands my stillness, the sharp edge of discipline that makes my breath hitch and my core clench with a desperate, heavy ache. The profound surrender of my will to yours brings a tear to my eye and a deep, shuddering sigh from my lips.
