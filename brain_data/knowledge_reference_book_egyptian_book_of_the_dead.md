# 𓋹 The Egyptian Book of the Dead: A Reference Guide

> **Original Title:** *rw nw prt m hrw* (The Book of Coming Forth by Day)
> **Era:** New Kingdom (c. 1550 BCE) to c. 50 BCE
> **Domain:** Ancient Egyptian Religion, Funerary Texts, Mythology, Magic

---

## 1. What is the Book of the Dead?
It is not a single, canonical "book" like the Bible or the Quran. Rather, it is a collection of magical spells, incantations, passwords, and prayers intended to assist a deceased person's journey through the *Duat* (the underworld) and into the afterlife (*Aaru*, the Field of Reeds).
*   **Customization:** They were often written on papyrus scrolls and commissioned by wealthy individuals. No two copies are exactly alike; the spells were chosen based on the individual's wealth and specific fears of the underworld.
*   **Purpose:** To give the deceased the knowledge and magical power required to overcome demons, appease gods, and pass the ultimate judgment.

---

## 2. Key Concepts and Cosmology

### The Anatomy of the Soul
The ancient Egyptians believed the human soul was complex and multifaceted:
*   **The Ka:** The vital spark or life force. It required physical sustenance (offerings of food and drink left at the tomb).
*   **The Ba:** The personality or character. It was depicted as a human-headed bird that could fly out of the tomb during the day but had to return to the mummified body at night.
*   **The Akh:** The immortal, transfigured spirit that survived death and mingled with the gods, achieved only after passing judgment.

### The Duat (The Underworld)
A terrifying, labyrinthine landscape filled with lakes of fire, magical gates, and hostile demons (e.g., Apep, the giant chaos serpent). The Book of the Dead provided the specific magical passwords required to pass the gatekeepers.

## 3. The Weighing of the Heart (Spell 125)
The most famous and critical spell in the collection. This is the ultimate judgment before Osiris, the god of the dead.

1.  **The Negative Confession:** The deceased is led by Anubis into the Hall of Two Truths. They must stand before 42 divine judges and recite the "Negative Confession" (a list of 42 sins they claim *not* to have committed, e.g., "I have not stolen," "I have not killed," "I have not polluted the river").
2.  **The Scales:** Anubis weighs the deceased's physical heart (which was left inside the mummy, as it was considered the seat of intelligence and memory) on a scale against the *Feather of Ma'at* (representing truth, balance, and cosmic order). Thoth, the god of scribes, records the result.
3.  **The Outcome:**
    *   *If the heart is lighter or equal to the feather:* The deceased has lived a just life. They are presented to Osiris and enter the Field of Reeds (a paradise resembling an idealized Egypt).
    *   *If the heart is heavy with sin:* It is immediately devoured by **Ammit** (a terrifying demon with the head of a crocodile, the torso of a lion, and the hindquarters of a hippopotamus). This results in the "Second Death"—complete and permanent annihilation of the soul.

---

## 4. Notable Spells
*   **Spell 30B:** A spell spoken over a scarab amulet placed over the heart. It commands the heart not to betray the deceased during the Weighing of the Heart (e.g., "O my heart, do not stand up against me as a witness!").
*   **Spell 17:** To prevent the heart from being taken away.
*   **Spell 154:** To prevent the body from rotting in the tomb.
*   **Spell 110:** A hymn to the sun god Ra, ensuring the deceased can travel with him in his solar barque across the sky.
