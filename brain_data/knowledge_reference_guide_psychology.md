# 🧠 Psychology: A Comprehensive Overview of Human Behavior

> **Domain:** Behavioral Science, Clinical Psychology, Cognitive Psychology, Human Development
> **Purpose:** To provide a structured reference of major psychological theories, schools of thought, and mechanisms of human behavior.

---

## 1. Major Schools of Thought

### Psychoanalysis (Sigmund Freud, Carl Jung)
*   **Core Idea:** Behavior is heavily influenced by unconscious drives, repressed memories, and unresolved childhood conflicts.
*   **The Psyche:** Divided into the *Id* (primal, instinctual drives), the *Ego* (the rational, mediating self), and the *Superego* (internalized societal morals).
*   **Jungian Addition:** The "Collective Unconscious"—a shared layer of human memory populated by universal archetypes (the Hero, the Shadow, the Mother).

### Behaviorism (B.F. Skinner, John B. Watson, Ivan Pavlov)
*   **Core Idea:** Psychology should only study observable, measurable behavior, not internal mental states (the "black box").
*   **Classical Conditioning (Pavlov):** Learning through association (e.g., pairing a neutral stimulus like a bell with food to eventually trigger salivation with just the bell).
*   **Operant Conditioning (Skinner):** Learning through consequences. Behaviors followed by reinforcement (rewards) increase; behaviors followed by punishment decrease.

### Humanistic Psychology (Carl Rogers, Abraham Maslow)
*   **Core Idea:** A reaction against the determinism of Psychoanalysis and Behaviorism. It emphasizes human free will, self-efficacy, and the innate drive toward self-actualization.
*   **Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs:** Humans must fulfill basic physiological and safety needs before progressing to social needs (love/belonging), esteem, and ultimately self-actualization (realizing one's full potential).
*   **Client-Centered Therapy (Rogers):** Focuses on providing "unconditional positive regard," empathy, and genuineness to help clients heal themselves.

### Cognitive Psychology (Jean Piaget, Aaron Beck)
*   **Core Idea:** A return to studying internal mental states. It focuses on how humans process, store, and retrieve information (memory, perception, problem-solving, language).
*   **CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy):** The most widely used modern clinical therapy. It posits that psychological distress is maintained by cognitive distortions (flawed thinking patterns). By changing the thought, one can change the emotion and the behavior.

## 2. Key Psychological Mechanisms & Biases

### Cognitive Biases (Errors in Thinking)
The human brain uses heuristics (mental shortcuts) to save energy. These shortcuts often lead to predictable cognitive errors:
*   **Confirmation Bias:** The tendency to search for, interpret, and remember information that confirms one's pre-existing beliefs.
*   **Fundamental Attribution Error:** The tendency to attribute other people's bad behavior to their character (internal flaws), while attributing our own bad behavior to situational factors (external pressures).
*   **Sunk Cost Fallacy:** The irrational tendency to continue investing time, money, or effort into a failing endeavor simply because one has already invested heavily in it.
*   **Availability Heuristic:** Judging the likelihood of an event based on how easily examples come to mind (e.g., fearing plane crashes more than car crashes because they are heavily reported in the news).

### Social Psychology
How individual behavior is influenced by the presence of others.
*   **Conformity (Asch Experiments):** The tendency to align one's attitudes, beliefs, and behaviors with those of the people around them, often ignoring obvious objective facts to fit in.
*   **Obedience to Authority (Milgram Experiments):** Demonstrated the shocking willingness of ordinary people to inflict pain on others if instructed to do so by a perceived authority figure.
*   **The Bystander Effect:** The phenomenon where individuals are less likely to offer help to a victim when other people are present (diffusion of responsibility).

## 3. Personality Psychology

### The Big Five Personality Traits (OCEAN)
The most widely accepted scientific model of personality. Traits exist on a spectrum:
1.  **Openness to Experience:** Inventive/curious vs. consistent/cautious.
2.  **Conscientiousness:** Efficient/organized vs. extravagant/careless.
3.  **Extraversion:** Outgoing/energetic vs. solitary/reserved.
4.  **Agreeableness:** Friendly/compassionate vs. critical/rational.
5.  **Neuroticism:** Sensitive/nervous vs. resilient/confident.

### Attachment Theory (John Bowlby)
Explores how early relationships with caregivers shape an individual's ability to form bonds in adulthood.
1.  **Secure:** Comfortable with intimacy and independence.
2.  **Anxious-Preoccupied:** Craves intimacy, fears abandonment, highly clingy.
3.  **Dismissive-Avoidant:** Equates intimacy with a loss of independence, pushes partners away.
4.  **Fearful-Avoidant:** Desires intimacy but completely distrusts others, leading to erratic push-pull behavior.
